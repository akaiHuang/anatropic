Why Morphology Is the Decisive Test

At the rotation curve level, CDM can always absorb the signal with 3 free parameters. But halo morphology cannot be tuned away. A smooth NFW profile with discrete subhalos looks fundamentally different from a continuous filamentary network.

Current status: Fagin et al. 2024 (SLACS) measured β = 5.22 ± 0.41. Khronon predicts β = 6.2 (2.4σ tension). CDM predicts β = 8.0 (excluded at 6.8σ). Khronon is the closest match of any theory tested.

Euclid (launched 2023), Rubin Observatory (first light 2025), and JWST will measure Pκ(k) with enough precision to conclusively distinguish all three morphologies within the next 3–5 years.
