Four Types of Structure from cs → 0

1. Sheets (Pancakes)

First structures to form. 1D gravitational collapse compresses matter into thin planar structures. In CDM, these are Zel'dovich pancakes. In Khronon, the same physics applies but ALL scales collapse simultaneously.

All 3 models

2. Filaments

Where sheets intersect. Matter flows along filaments toward nodes. Khronon prediction: continuous fluid filaments with ρfil/ρbg ~ L/λJ ~ 10–200. CDM has discrete subhalo chains instead.

Khronon signature

3. Nodes

Density peaks at filament intersections. In Khronon: 3D collapse with density contrast up to ~646× (from 64³ simulation at 3.5 tff). Galaxies and galaxy clusters sit at these nodes.

All 3 models

4. Voids

Underdense regions where matter has drained away. In the τ framework, voids have τ → 0 (nearly time-symmetric, weak arrow of time). The τ field provides a landscape of temporal asymmetry.

Why This Matters: The Morphology Is Testable

At galaxy rotation curve level, CDM (with 3 free parameters) can always absorb the signal. But the internal halo morphology cannot be tuned away. Khronon predicts continuous filamentary sub-structure with P(κ) ~ k−2.2 at convergence amplitude δκ ~ 10−3 to 10−2.

Current status: Fagin et al. 2024 (SLACS lensing) measured β = 5.22 ± 0.41. Khronon predicts β = 6.2 (2.4σ tension). CDM predicts β = 8.0 (excluded at 6.8σ). Khronon is the closest match of any theory tested.

Next-generation surveys (Euclid, JWST, Rubin Observatory) will measure Pκ(k) to sufficient precision to distinguish all three morphologies.
