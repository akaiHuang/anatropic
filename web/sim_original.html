The τ field: where time has direction

What τ represents

τ = 1 − exp(−Σ/2), where Σ = D(ρspacetime ‖ ρmatter) is the quantum relative entropy between spacetime geometry and matter content.

  • τ = 0: time-reversal symmetry intact. No arrow of time. The past can be perfectly recovered from the present.
  • τ → 1: complete irreversibility. Information about the past is lost. Time flows in one direction only.
  • In the weak gravitational field: Σ ≈ 2Φ/c², where Φ is the gravitational potential.

Three views

  • Density (purple-orange): where matter is. Filaments, nodes, and voids — the cosmic web, same as any standard simulation.
  • τ Field (blue-white-red): where time's arrow is strong. White/red = deep potential wells, strong irreversibility. Blue = voids, nearly reversible.
  • Combined: both at once. Color = τ (blue→red), brightness = density. Bright red = dense + irreversible. Dark = empty + reversible.

Physical interpretation

  • The cosmic web is not just a map of where matter is — it is a map of where time flows strongest.
  • Galaxy clusters sit at nodes where τ is highest: time is most irreversible, information is most trapped.
  • Cosmic voids have τ ≈ 0: time is nearly symmetric, retrodiction (recovering the past) is easiest.
  • Filaments are channels of intermediate τ along which matter — and temporal asymmetry — flows toward nodes.

Note: colors show relative τ distribution (contrast-enhanced). In the real cosmic web, τ ~ 10−5 (weak field). The structure is physically correct; absolute values are scaled for visualization.
