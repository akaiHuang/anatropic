The τ field: where time has direction

What τ represents

  • τ = 1 − F where F is the Petz recovery fidelity
  • τ = 0: perfect time-reversal symmetry (no arrow of time)
  • τ = 1: complete irreversibility (information fully lost)
  • In gravity: τ = 1 − exp(−|Φ|/Φmax)

What you see

  • Red regions: deep gravitational potential wells — strong time arrow, information trapped
  • Blue regions: voids — nearly reversible, weak time arrow
  • Filaments: intermediate τ, information flowing toward nodes

Physical interpretation

  • The Khronon field IS the time field — its gradient defines the preferred time direction
  • Jeans instability creates filamentary structure where τ varies spatially
  • This is a prediction: dark matter structures create a landscape of temporal asymmetry
