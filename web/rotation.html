Why this matters

The rotation curve problem

  • Stars in galaxies orbit faster than visible matter can explain
  • Standard cosmology (ΛCDM) posits invisible dark matter halos (NFW profile)
  • Each galaxy needs 2-3 free parameters to fit its halo — 525+ total for 175 galaxies
  • After 40+ years, no dark matter particle has been detected

The Khronon prediction

  • The τ framework predicts an acceleration scale a₀ = cH₀/(2π) from first principles
  • This equals 1.13 × 10⁻¹⁰ m/s² — within 6% of the empirical value (McGaugh 2016)
  • Only 1 free parameter per galaxy (mass-to-light ratio, physically motivated)
  • Zero "dark matter" parameters — the effect comes from spacetime entropy

How to read this chart

  • White dots: observed rotation velocities from radio + optical data
  • Gray dashed: baryonic (visible matter) contribution alone
  • Blue line: Khronon prediction — a₀ fixed, only M/L fitted
  • Red line: best-fit NFW halo — 3 parameters adjusted per galaxy
  • Where blue matches white without red's extra freedom: Khronon wins
